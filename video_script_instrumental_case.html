Part 1 — The Instrumental Case with Цікавитися & Займатися

Segments covering the two verbs that require the Instrumental case, with conjugation examples and real-life scenarios.

Segment 1 Introduction — Two verbs obsessed with the Instrumental

On screen

Цікавитися
Займатися

Привіт! You already know how the Instrumental case can help us at a café or restaurant. Now, there are two specific verbs that are obsessed with the Instrumental case: Цікавитися (to be interested in) and Займатися (to engage in / practice). If you use these verbs, you must use the Instrumental. No excuses!

Segment 2 Цікавитися — To be interested in

On screen

Цікавитися
Я цікавлюся історією
Історія → історією

Цікавитися means "to be interested in." Notice that in English we say "interested in," but in Ukrainian you don't need a preposition — you just put the noun in the Instrumental.

Example: Я цікавлюся історією. (I am interested in history.)
Grammar check: Історія (feminine) changed to історією.

Segment 3 Займатися — To do / practice / be occupied with

On screen

Займатися
Він займається бізнесом.
Ми займаємося спортом.

Займатися is a bit of a chameleon — it can mean "to practice," "to do," or "to be occupied with." We use it for sports, hobbies, or even work.

Він займається бізнесом. He does business / He is occupied with business. — Бізнес → бізнесом
Ми займаємося спортом. We practice sports. — Спорт → спортом
Segment 4 Real-life scenarios — Both verbs together

On screen — split screen: photography / yoga

Чим ти цікавишся?
Я цікавлюся фотографією. А ще я займаюся йогою.

Imagine you're at a party in Kyiv and someone asks: Чим ти цікавишся? (What are you interested in?)

You could say:
Я цікавлюся фотографією. (I'm interested in photography.)
А ще я займаюся йогою. (And I also practice yoga.)

Фотографія → фотографією (feminine -ою)
Йога → йогою (feminine -ою)
Segment 5 Pro tip — Займатися with people: add з

On screen

Я займаюся йогою з другом.

When you use займатися with people, add the preposition з (with): Я займаюся йогою з другом (I do yoga with a friend). But when it's an activity or a tool, you just use the case ending alone.

Segment 6 Your challenge

Try to form one sentence about yourself using one of these verbs. Do you practice karate? Are you interested in music?

Ukrainian grammar can feel like a puzzle, but once you snap these pieces together, everything starts to flow.

Part 2 — Грати на + Locative Case

How to talk about playing musical instruments in Ukrainian, including consonant mutation and the irregular піаніно.

Segment 7 Введення — Грати + на + Locative

On screen

Грати (To play) + на (on) + Locative Case

So, you want to tell your Ukrainian friends that you play the guitar? In English, you "play the guitar." Simple, right? But in Ukrainian, we don't just play it — we play on it.

Segment 8 The formula

On screen — large bold text

Грати + на + [Instrument]і
Грати + на + [Instrument]і

When talking about musical instruments, the formula is simple: use the preposition на (on) followed by the instrument in the Locative case. Think of it like this: you are performing on the surface of the music.

Segment 9 Endings — Most instruments take -і
Я граю на саксофоні Саксофон → саксофоні — I play the saxophone
Ти граєш на барабані Барабан → барабані — You play the drum
Він грає на гітарі Гітара → гітарі — He plays the guitar
Ми граємо на кобзі Кобза → кобзі — We play the kobza

Most instruments will end in -і in the Locative case.

Segment 10 ⚠️ Consonant mutation — К → Ц before -і

Watch out for the К sounds! In Ukrainian grammar, some letters transform when they meet the letter -і. This is called mutation. The К changes to Ц.

⚡
Скрипка (violin) → Вона грає на скрипці
Сопілка (flute/pipe) → Ви граєте на сопілці
К mutates to Ц before Locative -і
Segment 11 The rebel — Піаніно doesn't change

On screen

Піаніно
Я граю на піаніно

And then there's the Piano / Піаніно. It's a foreign loanword, so it's "rebellious" and doesn't change its ending at all. It's always just піаніно.

Я граю на піаніно — No change!

Segment 13 Your challenge — Який твій улюблений інструмент?

Remember: На is your best friend when music is involved. Try to answer:
Який твій улюблений інструмент? (What is your favourite instrument?)

Now you can complete sentences like:
Я граю на… (I play the…)
Я хочу грати на… (I want to play the…)

📋 Full Recap

1 The Instrumental Case is your "tool" or "hobby" case. Masculine/Neuter → -ом, -ем, -єм. Feminine → -ою, -ею, -єю.
2 Цікавитися = to be interested in → always takes the Instrumental, no preposition needed.
3 Займатися = to do / practice / be occupied with → Instrumental for activities; add з + Instrumental for people.
4 Formula for musical instruments: Грати + на + [Instrument]і
5 Watch for К → Ц mutation before Locative -і (скрипка → скрипці, сопілка → сопілці).
6 Foreign loanwords like піаніно are invariable — no ending change.
